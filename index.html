Living In San Diego

I lived in San Diego from the fall of 2012 until December, 2018. I decided to move back to the Saint Louis area so that I could start a better career and be closer to family. I decided to make my move home a road trip. I traveled by myself to a few different places.

Zion National Park, Utah

My first stop on my trip home was Zion National Park, which is located in Utah. I woke up at 4am and drove about 10 hours to get to the park before sunset and was able to go for a short hike before it got dark. I was disappointed that I didnt' have more time to spend there.

Monument Valley

Another one of my stops was Monument Valley, located on the Utah-Arizona border. It has been featured in many westerns. The park is on Navajo land. I spent a few hours driving around to see all of the different rock formations. After Monument Valley I continued driving until I got to Page, Arizona.

Horseshoe Bend

The next morning, I woke up early again to see the sunrise at Horseshoe Bend. There were signs everywhere warning visitors to stay away from the dangerous cliff edges, but many tourists were in search of the "perfect" picture. I was just fine getting a shot from within the signs.

Arches National Park

My last fun stop on my trip was Arches National Park, located in Utah. I drove around the park a little bit, but by that time most things were closed due to the government shut down. I hiked Delicate Arch at sunset and managed to make it back to my car before sunset. When I tried to leave the park, I came to a road closure--someone had accidentally driven into a steep ravine and had to get towed out. I got stuck in the park well after dark. I drove until about 2am, woke up at 7am the next morning, and drove over 12 hours to get back to Southern Illinois.

Life in Southern Illinois

When I moved back to the area I started out as a substitute teacher in a local school district since I have a teaching background. I eventually got in touch with a contracting company and was offered a 3 month position at Clayco as a an IT Executive Assistant. I was eventually hired on full time as a Business Analyst. I have also been able to spend a lot more time with my family that lives in the area, including my mom, brother, and sister.
